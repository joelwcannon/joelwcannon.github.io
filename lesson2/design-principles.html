Design Principles

Fitt's Law

mint.com

Fitt’s Law states that buttons that are larger and closer are more likely to be clicked. Note how Intuit places a Large, clearly labeled, colorful button immediately below the last item on the registration form. Each textbox is ordered sequentially, so it is clear what to do next. The alternate Sign in button is much smaller and above the form. All of the buttons are blue, so they are easy to identify. But size and location is used according to Fitt’s Law to encourage new registrations.

Proximity

audible.com

Audible has a large list of audio books, so presenting lots of information is a challenge. Here you see how they organize books in rows with a cover, title and referral all grouped close together. The next row is separated by a subtitle, making it clear that those are different books. The white space also gathers all the related information together.

Repetition

mint.com

Mint repeats colors, fonts and buttons to focus your attention and help you get started. The orange Sign up Free button is repeated at the top of the page. The sign-up/in buttons are repeated with different colors. The teal color from the logo is repeated to focus your attention. And the two fonts are repeated consistently.

Visual Heirarchy

wolframalpha.com

WolframAlpha uses colors, titles, boxes and columns to create a hierarchy of information. All of the orange Mathematics topics are clearly grouped. The menu options at the top are placed in their own hierarchy.

Alignment

domains.google.com

Google Domains uses alignment to structure and organize their page. Rows and columns group information.clearly.The page looks neat and orderly. This gives you confidence that they are professional and will provide consistent service.

Contrast

translate.google.com

Google Translate uses contrasting backgrounds to separate the translated inputs and outputs. The text is presented in colors that contrast the backgrounds, making them easy to read. Contrasting fonts are used to focus your attention. High and low contrast icons help you understand which are active.
